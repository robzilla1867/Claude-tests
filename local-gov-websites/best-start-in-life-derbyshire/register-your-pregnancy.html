The earlier you let your midwifery team know you're pregnant, the earlier we can start planning your care with you.

You can self-refer to Derbyshire's community midwifery service as soon as you get a positive pregnancy test — you do not need to see your GP first.

When you register, we'll ask a few questions about your health and general wellbeing, and arrange your first booking appointment. This usually happens before you're 10 weeks pregnant.

What happens next

  1. Complete the online self-referral form. It takes about 10 minutes, and you can save it and come back to it if you need to.
  2. A midwife will contact you within 3 working days to arrange your booking appointment.
  3. At your booking appointment, we'll talk through your health and history, offer some routine tests, and start your pregnancy notes.

If you're not sure where to start

That's OK. You can call the Derbyshire Maternity Booking Line and a member of the team will talk you through what to do next, whatever stage you're at.

Start your self-referral

Enter your details below and a member of our midwifery team will contact you within 3 working days to arrange your first appointment.
